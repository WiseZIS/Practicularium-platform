In recent years, through conversations with practitioners in science, business, and creative fields, I increasingly received the same feedback: knowledge, ideas, and experience must be brought into open discussion.
Only there do they gain scale — through feedback, critique, development, and the overall growth of a direction.
That is why I made the decision not merely to document my practices, but to share them openly.
This is how the book «Practicularium» was born — because a book is a source of knowledge that can be transmitted across time.
Today,«racticularium» — is:
  • the results of real-life practices
  • my reflections on human nature, existence, and consciousness
  • approaches to management and decision analysis
  • original author-developed models
  • practical tools and methods
This is not theory and not an empty motivation. Not dogma and not a demand. It is a practical product.
Documented experience. Recommendations and tools that have been tested in real life.
The book is published in three languages — not by accident:
  • Ukrainian — has rightfully regained its primacy in Ukraine. Today, most public dialogue is conducted in Ukrainian — and increasingly, it is the language in which people think!
  • English — as an international language, it connects with many European countries with which I would primarily like to build future development and practical collaboration.
  • «Rus’kiy» — in my personal interpretation, I associate this term with its historical root, “Rus’,” which by definition was Kyivan.
Additional translations into Polish, Italian, German, French, and Spanish are also planned.
